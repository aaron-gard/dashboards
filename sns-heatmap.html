💡 Strategic Insights

1. Dual-channel network is a strength. Unlike Review (81% Myer-dependent), SNS has a healthier mix: 40 own stores plus 56 Myer concessions. This means brand experience isn't solely dependent on a wholesale partner.

2. Repeat rate is the killer metric. 52% repeat within 5km is extraordinary. Parents buying kids shoes every season creates a predictable, recurring revenue base. Every new store location unlocks this flywheel.

3. Need-based purchase = distance-resilient AOV. The flat $107 AOV proves kids shoes are bought by need (growing feet), not desire. But repeat rate drops 10 points with distance, showing stores drive frequency, not basket size.

4. 69% within 10km is high. Higher than Review's 62%. This suggests SNS customers are even more proximity-driven, likely because parents prefer in-store fitting for children's footwear.
