üîç Strategic Insights

Revenue recovery is channel-dependent, not organic growth
‚ñæ
FY26 YTD tRS revenue is $82.5M vs $78.7M prior year (+4.8%), but this masks a structural shift. Concession surged +20% ($49.0M), while Boutique collapsed ‚àí38% ($19.5M) and Outlets ‚àí36% ($9.2M). Online channels are NOT one story: DTC +77% ($3.6M), Myer Online +93% ($1.9M), The Iconic +191% ($369K off a small base), DJs Online $175K (new). The DTC growth is real, but Myer Online nearly doubled ‚Äî Review is increasingly a marketplace brand. Recommendation: Track DTC, Myer Online, and Iconic separately. Blended "online" figures hide the dependency mix.
Store closures are destroying demand, not just shifting it online
‚ñ∏
84 of 165 locations (51%) are now closed. Analysis of closure catchments shows ‚àí40-60% online revenue decline in affected postcodes, ‚àí55% new signups, and zero evidence of online compensation. Physical stores drive digital awareness. Each closure permanently destroys local demand. Recommendation: Model the revenue impact before any further closures. Consider pop-ups or partnerships to maintain local presence.
Dress Circle tier is a design failure, not a geography problem
‚ñ∏
50-100 Dress Circle members downgrade every quarter. Only 191 remain from a peak of 695 in 2021. Analysis shows only 3 of 191 remaining DC members live in store closure catchments. The churn is driven by the tier renewal mechanism requiring sustained high spend, not store closures. Net negative every year since 2021 (‚àí28, ‚àí186, ‚àí143, ‚àí85). Recommendation: Redesign tier renewal criteria ‚Äî consider lifetime value or engagement metrics, not just annual spend thresholds.
37.8% of transacting members are one-time buyers
‚ñ∏
Of 95,627 members who transacted in the last 12 months, 36,134 (37.8%) made only one purchase. A further 24,285 (25.4%) made exactly two. Only 4,672 members (4.9%) are high-frequency (10+ transactions). The loyalty program is failing to convert trial into repeat. Recommendation: Implement post-first-purchase engagement sequence within 30 days. Target the 36K one-timers with personalized reactivation.
Signups declining sharply ‚Äî acquisition engine failing
‚ñ∏
FY26 YTD signups are running ~22% below prior year (ex. the Dec 2024 anomaly of 21,633 ‚Äî likely a data import). Monthly signups have dropped from 5,000-6,000 range in 2023 to 3,000-4,000 in 2025. Jan 2026 was just 2,977 ‚Äî the lowest non-anomaly month in 3 years. Recommendation: Audit signup capture at remaining locations. Ensure online checkout captures loyalty enrollment.
Redemptions declining ‚Äî engagement loop weakening
‚ñ∏
Monthly redemptions dropped from ~5,700 avg in H1 2024 to ~2,900 avg in H2 2025. CY25 total of 36,781 vs CY24 of 50,491 ‚Äî a 27% decline. Fewer redemptions means less perceived value, which accelerates member disengagement. Recommendation: Review reward catalog attractiveness. Consider surprise-and-delight mechanics.
Concession channel performing strongly
‚ñ∏
Concession (Myer) revenue grew +20% YoY to $49.0M, accounting for 49% of all loyalty revenue. Myer Melbourne ($243 AOV), Myer Maroochydore ($297 AOV), and Myer Pitt Street ($183 AOV) are standout performers. This channel is partially offsetting boutique losses. Recommendation: Invest in top Myer concessions. Negotiate for better positions/space in high-performing stores.
Online channel growing rapidly but from a low base
‚ñ∏
Online loyalty revenue grew +92% FY26 YTD ($6.1M vs $3.2M), but the channels tell different stories: DTC $3.6M (+77%) is the core, Myer Online $1.9M (+93%) nearly doubled, The Iconic $369K (+191%) tripled off a tiny base, and DJs Online $175K is new. Myer Online is now 31% of all online loyalty revenue ‚Äî marketplace dependency is growing. Recommendation: Monitor DTC share of online. If Myer Online keeps growing faster, the brand is renting its customer relationship.
63% of online signups never buy ‚Äî physical stores convert at 96%
‚ñ∏
Physical store signups (Boutique + Concession) convert to paying customers at 96%. Online signups convert at just 37% ‚Äî 63% never buy anything. When online members DO transact, they actually spend more ($443 lifetime, $145 AOV) than physical ($358-$419 lifetime). The problem isn't value per customer ‚Äî it's that most online signups aren't customers at all. Physical stores are acquisition engines with near-perfect conversion. Recommendation: Reframe store ROI to include acquisition conversion rate, not just in-store revenue.
69% never return ‚Äî 7-day window is critical for second purchase
‚ñ∏
69.1% of members never make a second purchase. Of the 30.9% who do return, 8% come back within 7 days ‚Äî the single biggest conversion window. Only 14.4% return within 30 days. After 90 days, probability of return drops sharply. Recommendation: Deploy aggressive post-purchase engagement (email/SMS) within the first week. A "welcome back" offer at day 3-5 could dramatically boost repeat rates.
Legacy members drive 49.5% of FY26 revenue ‚Äî retention is critical
‚ñ∏
FY26 YTD revenue breakdown: 49.5% ($8.1M) from members acquired before FY23, 33.8% ($5.5M) from brand-new FY26 members, and 16.7% from FY23-FY25 cohorts. The loyalty base is the revenue engine ‚Äî members acquired 3+ years ago still drive half of all revenue. Recommendation: Each churned legacy member represents outsized revenue loss. Prioritise retention.
Multi-channel members spend 2.5√ó more on a 12-month basis ‚Äî $560 vs $223
‚ñ∏
Members who shop both online and in-store spend $560/year vs $223 for physical-only and $269 for online-only (12-month active members). On a lifetime basis, multi-channel members accumulate $4,069 over 28 transactions. They need physical stores to exist. Recommendation: Converting single-channel to multi-channel should be a priority. Incentivise cross-channel visits ‚Äî every physical customer who buys online becomes 2.5√ó more valuable.
High-value members concentrated at top ‚Äî fragile base
‚ñ∏
13.5% of transacting members (12,905) spend $1,000+ annually, while 15.7% (15,016) spend under $100. The top spenders are critical ‚Äî losing even a small percentage has outsized revenue impact. With Dress Circle declining and only 4,915 members in Tiers 2-3, the high-value member pipeline is thinning. Recommendation: Implement VIP retention program for $500+ spenders independent of tier status.
